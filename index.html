OmniCam

Abstract

Camera Control, which achieves diverse visual effects by changing camera position and orientation, has attracted widespread attention. However, existing methods face challenges such as complex interaction and limited control capabilities. To address these issues, we present OmniCam, a unified multimodal camera control framework. Leveraging large language models and video diffusion models, OmniCam generates spatio-temporally consistent videos. It supports various combinations of input modalities: users can provide text or videos with expected trajectories as camera path guidance, and images or videos as content references, enabling precise control over camera motion. To facilitate the training of OmniCam, we introduce the OmniTr dataset, which contains a large collection of high-quality long-sequence trajectories, videos, and corresponding descriptions. Experimental results demonstrate that our model achieves state-of-the-art performance in high-quality camera-controlled video generation across various metrics. Our code and demonstrations will be available at this page.

OmniCam

  • Hunyuan only supports text as the content reference; experiments show it lacks the ability to control camera motion.
  • MotionMaster also supports only text as the content reference, and its camera motion control performance is unsatisfactory.
  • OmniCam is a good work, trained on the RealEstate dataset, which has inspired a lot of subsequent research. However, its generalization is insufficient and we verified the code in its official library. From our experimental results, selecting existing camera trajectories from the RealEstate dataset works well (the official inference code loads trajectories from the RealEstate dataset). Probably because the RealEstate dataset contains more camera forward effects, it works well forward and to a small extent backward. But when we customize the trajectory, e.g. “backward and right”, its spatial structure is destroyed, as shown in the video. From a technical point of view, it is less difficult to move the camera forward and to both sides at the same time than to move directly to the right or backward. This is because the input image itself contains information about the complete picture, and by advancing forward and then moving to the right, the process requires less unknown information to be added. Nonetheless, this is still not a bad piece of work and we appreciate its contribution to the community.
  • CogVideoX allows images as the content reference, but its ability to control camera motion remains inadequate.
  • Although Runway is currently the best method for controlling camera movement, it only supports simple camera operations.
  • OmniCam demonstrates the effect of its handling of camera rotational movement here.
